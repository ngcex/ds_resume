On 4 August 2020, a catastrophic explosion at the Port of Beirut killed more than 200 people, injured thousands, and destroyed a large part of the city's medical infrastructure — including the main hospital near the blast site.

Through my involvement with **Rotaract** (the Rotary International youth service organisation), I served as the **ERIC country representative for Italy** in a cross-border humanitarian initiative called *Drugs & Help for Lebanon*. ERIC (European Rotaract Information Centre) coordinated Rotaract clubs across Europe to collect and ship urgently needed medicines and medical supplies to Beirut in the weeks following the explosion.

My role involved coordinating the Italian contribution: mobilising local clubs, organising the collection of donated pharmaceuticals, and liaising with the European logistics chain to ensure the supplies reached the right destination.

The experience reinforced the value of distributed volunteer networks for rapid humanitarian response — and the particular effectiveness of peer-to-peer coordination across borders when institutional channels are slow.
