On 4 August 2020, a catastrophic explosion at the Port of Beirut killed more than 200 people, injured over 7,000, left an estimated 300,000 homeless, and destroyed a large part of the city's medical infrastructure — including the hospital closest to the blast.

In the weeks that followed, **Rotaract** clubs from Sicily and Lebanon joined forces in a cross-border humanitarian initiative called *Drugs & Help for Lebanon*. The project was coordinated through **ERIC** (European Rotaract Information Centre), the body that links Rotaract districts across Europe, and brought together Italian and Lebanese volunteers to organise the collection and shipment of urgently needed medicines and medical supplies to Beirut.

As the **ERIC country representative for Italy**, my role was to coordinate the Italian contribution: mobilising Rotaract clubs across the country, organising the collection of donated pharmaceuticals, and liaising with the European logistics chain to ensure supplies reached the right destination. The initiative was led together with Jean-Marc Adaimi (Lebanon), Massimo Casamichele, Federica Ferrara, and Beatrice Raffagnino (Italy).

The experience highlighted how distributed peer-to-peer volunteer networks can mount a rapid humanitarian response when institutional channels are slow — and how personal connections across borders become critical infrastructure in a crisis.
